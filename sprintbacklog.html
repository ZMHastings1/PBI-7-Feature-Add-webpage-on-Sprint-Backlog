    Sprint vs Product Backlog

What is the difference between a Sprint Backlog and a Product Backlog?

The Product Backlog is the single, ordered list of everything that might improve the product: features, fixes, spikes, and technical work. It is long-lived and continuously refined as the team learns from users and stakeholders. Items are prioritized by value, risk, and learning potential, and they may change size and detail over time.

The Sprint Backlog is short-term and Sprint-specific. During Sprint Planning, the Scrum Team selects the highest-value Product Backlog items and crafts a concrete plan to achieve a clear Sprint Goal. These items are broken into actionable tasks that the Developers manage day-to-day. The Sprint Backlog can evolve as new work is discovered, but it always reflects the team’s current plan to meet the Sprint Goal.

Example: A Product Backlog might include “guest checkout,” “improve search relevance,” and “reduce checkout latency.” For the current Sprint, the team chooses “improve search relevance” and creates a Sprint Backlog of tasks like “add query expansion,” “tune ranking,” and “write A/B test plan.” The remaining items stay on the Product Backlog for future Sprints.
